Albert Einstein: A Legendary Physicist

Early Life

Albert Einstein was born on March 14, 1879, in Ulm, Kingdom of Württemberg, German Empire. He was a theoretical physicist widely known for developing the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics.

Major Achievements

  • Developed the theory of relativity, most famous for the equation E = mc2.
  • Received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
  • Made significant contributions to quantum theory and statistical mechanics.

Personal Life

Albert Einstein was married twice, first to Mileva Marić and later to Elsa Löwenthal. He had two sons and one daughter. Outside of his scientific pursuits, Einstein was known for his political activism, pacifism, and advocacy for civil rights.

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. For knowledge is limited, whereas imagination embraces the entire world, stimulating progress, giving birth to evolution." – Albert Einstein
